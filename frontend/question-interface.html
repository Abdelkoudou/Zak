Question #9
A 45-year-old male patient presents with chest pain and shortness of breath. The ECG shows ST-elevation in leads II, III, and aVF. Based on the anatomical location, which coronary artery is most likely occluded?
Medical Image/Diagram (ECG, X-ray, etc.)
A
Left Anterior Descending (LAD) artery
B
Right Coronary Artery (RCA)
C
Left Circumflex (LCX) artery
D
Posterior Descending Artery (PDA)
Detailed Explanation
Correct Answer: B - Right Coronary Artery (RCA)

ST-elevation in leads II, III, and aVF indicates an inferior wall myocardial infarction. These leads look at the inferior (diaphragmatic) surface of the heart, which is primarily supplied by the Right Coronary Artery (RCA) in most patients (85-90%).

Key Points:
  • Leads II, III, aVF = Inferior wall
  • RCA supplies inferior wall in right-dominant systems
  • Associated findings may include AV blocks due to RCA supplying SA and AV nodes
  • Consider checking posterior leads (V7-V9) for posterior extension
